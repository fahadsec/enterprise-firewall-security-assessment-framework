# 1. Purpose

This standard establishes the mandatory reporting requirements for all security assessments performed under the Enterprise Firewall Security Assessment Framework (EFSAF).

The objective of this document is to ensure that every assessment report produced by internal security teams, external consultants, auditors, managed security providers, and third-party assessors follows a standardized enterprise reporting methodology.

Consistent reporting enables organizations to:

* Produce repeatable security assessments.
* Improve executive decision making.
* Maintain assessment consistency across multiple teams.
* Support regulatory compliance.
* Improve remediation management.
* Enable historical comparison of security posture.
* Reduce reporting ambiguity.
* Improve audit defensibility.
* Establish measurable security governance.

This standard shall be applied to every EFSAF assessment regardless of organization size, technology vendor, industry sector, or regulatory environment.

---

# 3. Objectives

Assessment reports generated under EFSAF shall achieve the following objectives.

## 3.1 Accuracy

Reports shall accurately represent the observed security posture without exaggeration, omission, or unsupported conclusions.

---

## 3.2 Evidence-Based Reporting

Every finding shall be supported by verifiable evidence.

Opinions shall never be reported as confirmed findings.

---

## 3.3 Repeatability

Independent assessors reviewing the same environment should reach substantially similar conclusions when applying EFSAF standards.

---

## 3.4 Traceability

Every reported finding shall be traceable to:

* Assessment evidence
* Assessment methodology
* Risk evaluation
* Applicable control
* Recommendation
* Supporting documentation

---

## 3.5 Executive Communication

Reports shall communicate technical risks in language understandable by executive leadership while maintaining sufficient technical detail for engineering teams.

---

## 3.6 Actionability

Every finding shall include clear remediation guidance enabling corrective action.

Reports should avoid reporting issues that cannot be reasonably acted upon.

---

## 3.7 Consistency

All EFSAF reports shall follow the reporting structure defined in this standard.

Organizations may extend report content but shall not remove mandatory sections.

---

# 4. Reporting Principles

All EFSAF reports shall adhere to the following reporting principles.

## 4.1 Objectivity

Assessment reports shall remain objective.

Personal opinions, assumptions, speculation, or unsupported conclusions shall not appear within official reports.

---

## 4.2 Independence

Assessment conclusions shall remain independent from:

* Product vendors
* Technology partners
* Commercial interests
* Internal political considerations
* Operational preferences

---

## 4.3 Evidence Integrity

Reported evidence shall remain:

* Accurate
* Complete
* Unmodified
* Verifiable
* Properly referenced

Evidence manipulation is prohibited.

---

## 4.4 Reproducibility

Another qualified assessor should be capable of reproducing reported observations using the documented methodology.

---

## 4.5 Clarity

Reports shall avoid ambiguous language.

Terms such as:

* "Maybe"
* "Probably"
* "Likely"
* "Seems"
* "Appears"

should not be used unless explicitly supported by documented evidence.

---

## 4.6 Risk-Oriented Reporting

Assessment reports shall prioritize business risk over technical complexity.

The significance of findings shall be communicated in terms of:

* Business impact
* Operational impact
* Security exposure
* Compliance implications
* Recovery implications

---

## 4.7 Vendor Neutrality

Reports shall not favor or criticize any technology vendor unless supported by objective technical evidence.

Recommendations shall focus on security outcomes rather than specific commercial products.

---

## 4.8 Professional Language

Assessment reports shall use formal professional language.

Reports shall avoid:

* Emotional wording
* Marketing language
* Product promotion
* Informal terminology
* Personal commentary

---

# 7. Standard Enterprise Report Structure

All EFSAF assessment reports **shall** follow the standardized report structure defined in this section. Organizations may extend the report with additional sections; however, mandatory sections shall not be omitted or reordered without documented justification.

The recommended report structure is as follows:

| Section              | Mandatory | Purpose                                  |
| -------------------- | --------- | ---------------------------------------- |
| Cover Page           | Yes       | Report identification and classification |
| Document Control     | Yes       | Version history and approvals            |
| Table of Contents    | Yes       | Navigation                               |
| Executive Summary    | Yes       | High-level overview for leadership       |
| Assessment Overview  | Yes       | Scope, objectives, methodology           |
| Environment Overview | Yes       | Assessed environment summary             |
| Assessment Results   | Yes       | Summary of findings                      |
| Detailed Findings    | Yes       | Individual technical findings            |
| Risk Analysis        | Yes       | Business and technical risk evaluation   |
| Recommendations      | Yes       | Corrective actions                       |
| Remediation Roadmap  | Yes       | Prioritized remediation plan             |
| Compliance Mapping   | Yes       | Regulatory alignment                     |
| Conclusion           | Yes       | Overall assessment outcome               |
| Appendices           | Yes       | Supporting material                      |

# 15. Writing Standards for Findings

Each finding shall:

* Be evidence-based.
* Be technically accurate.
* Avoid assumptions.
* Clearly describe the issue.
* Explain the associated risk.

# 16. Evidence Presentation Standard

All assessment findings shall be supported by sufficient, reliable, and verifiable evidence in accordance with **EFSAF-EVD-001**.

Evidence included within an assessment report shall enable an independent reviewer to validate the reported observations without requiring assumptions or undocumented information.

Evidence shall be directly relevant to the associated finding and shall accurately reflect the assessed environment at the time of the assessment.

---

## 16.1 Evidence Requirements

Every finding shall reference one or more forms of supporting evidence.

Acceptable evidence includes, but is not limited to:

* Firewall configuration extracts
* Rulebase exports
* Administrative interface screenshots
* Command-line output
* System logs
* Security event logs
* Audit logs
* Packet captures (PCAP)
* Network topology diagrams
* Architecture documentation
* Change management records
* Access control records
* Vulnerability assessment results
* Compliance documentation
* Asset inventory records
* Configuration backups

Evidence shall be clearly identified and traceable to the associated finding.

---

## 16.2 Evidence Referencing

Each evidence item shall be assigned a unique identifier.

Example:

```text
Evidence ID:
EVD-001

Type:
Firewall Configuration Export

Source:
Firewall Management Platform

Collection Date:
2026-06-10

Collected By:
Assessment Team
```

Evidence identifiers shall remain consistent throughout the report.

---

## 16.3 Evidence Integrity

Assessment evidence shall remain:

* Complete
* Accurate
* Authentic
* Time-stamped where possible
* Read-only after collection
* Protected against unauthorized modification

Evidence handling shall comply with organizational information security policies and applicable legal or regulatory requirements.

---

## 16.4 Sensitive Information

Reports shall minimize the exposure of sensitive information.

Where appropriate, the following shall be masked or redacted:

* Public IP addresses (where disclosure presents unnecessary risk)
* Internal IP addressing schemes
* Usernames
* Passwords
* Shared secrets
* API keys
* Certificates and private keys
* Customer data
* Personally Identifiable Information (PII)
* Protected Health Information (PHI)
* Financial information

Redactions shall not compromise the technical validity of the evidence.

---

# 17. Risk Communication Standard

Assessment reports shall communicate risk in a clear, consistent, and business-focused manner.

Technical observations shall be translated into business impact to support informed decision-making by executive leadership.

Risk communication shall align with **EFSAF-RISK-001**.

---

## 17.1 Risk Statement

Every finding shall include a structured risk statement describing:

* The identified weakness
* The affected assets
* The potential threat
* The potential business impact
* The expected security consequence

Example:

> "The absence of multi-factor authentication for firewall administrative access increases the likelihood of unauthorized administrative access, potentially resulting in unauthorized policy modification, disruption of critical services, and compromise of enterprise network security."

---

## 17.2 Business Impact

Business impact shall be evaluated across relevant organizational domains, including:

* Confidentiality
* Integrity
* Availability
* Regulatory compliance
* Financial impact
* Operational continuity
* Reputational impact
* Customer trust
* Service availability
* Recovery complexity

---

## 17.3 Technical Impact

Reports should describe the technical consequences of each finding, including:

* Increased attack surface
* Privilege escalation opportunities
* Lateral movement potential
* Policy bypass
* Reduced visibility
* Inadequate monitoring
* Weak administrative controls
* Configuration drift
* Reduced resilience

---

## 17.4 Risk Prioritization

Risk prioritization shall consider multiple factors, including:

* Likelihood of exploitation
* Potential business impact
* Exposure duration
* Asset criticality
* Existing compensating controls
* Threat landscape
* Regulatory implications
* Operational dependencies

Risk prioritization shall not rely solely on vulnerability severity scores.

---

# 18. Remediation Planning Standard

Every assessment report shall include a structured remediation plan.

Recommendations shall be practical, measurable, achievable, and prioritized according to organizational risk.

---

## 18.1 Recommendation Requirements

Every recommendation shall:

* Address the root cause
* Reduce identified risk
* Be technically feasible
* Be vendor-neutral where practical
* Support long-term security improvement
* Minimize operational disruption

Recommendations should avoid unnecessary complexity.

---

## 18.2 Remediation Priority

Each finding shall include a remediation priority.

Example:

| Priority  | Expected Response                               |
| --------- | ----------------------------------------------- |
| Immediate | Emergency remediation required                  |
| High      | Address in the next approved maintenance window |
| Medium    | Include in planned security improvements        |
| Low       | Address through routine operational activities  |

Organizations may define target remediation timeframes consistent with internal governance requirements.

---

## 18.3 Compensating Controls

Where immediate remediation is not feasible, the report shall identify appropriate compensating controls.

Compensating controls shall:

* Reduce residual risk
* Be documented
* Be approved by the appropriate risk owner
* Be periodically reviewed for effectiveness

---

# 19. Report Quality Assurance

All EFSAF assessment reports shall undergo formal quality assurance before release.

Quality assurance activities shall verify:

* Technical accuracy
* Completeness
* Consistency
* Grammar and formatting
* Evidence traceability
* Risk alignment
* Recommendation quality
* Compliance mapping
* Document integrity

Reports shall not be released until mandatory quality assurance activities have been completed.

---

## 19.1 Technical Review

A qualified reviewer independent of the primary assessor shall perform a technical review.

The reviewer shall verify:

* Technical findings
* Supporting evidence
* Risk ratings
* Remediation recommendations
* Compliance references

---

## 19.2 Editorial Review

Reports should undergo an editorial review to ensure:

* Consistent terminology
* Professional language
* Correct grammar
* Formatting consistency
* Table numbering
* Figure numbering
* Cross-reference accuracy

---

## 19.3 Management Approval

Prior to publication, the report shall receive formal approval from the designated assessment authority.

Approval shall confirm that:

* Assessment objectives were achieved.
* Findings are evidence-based.
* Risk ratings are justified.
* Recommendations are appropriate.
* The report complies with EFSAF standards.

---

# 20. Report Distribution and Protection

Assessment reports frequently contain sensitive security information and shall be protected throughout their lifecycle.

Organizations shall establish controls governing report distribution, storage, transmission, and disposal.

---

## 20.1 Distribution Controls

Report distribution shall be limited to authorized recipients.

Recipients may include:

* Executive management
* Information Security leadership
* Infrastructure management
* Network engineering teams
* Internal audit
* Compliance functions
* Authorized third-party stakeholders

Distribution shall follow the organization's information classification policy.

---

## 20.2 Secure Storage

Assessment reports shall be stored in approved repositories protected by appropriate access controls.

Stored reports should support:

* Version control
* Access logging
* Backup and recovery
* Integrity verification
* Retention management

---

## 20.3 Retention

Organizations shall define report retention periods in accordance with:

* Legal obligations
* Regulatory requirements
* Contractual commitments
* Internal governance policies
* Business continuity requirements

Expired reports shall be securely archived or destroyed using approved information disposal procedures.

# 21. Report Lifecycle Management

Assessment reports shall be managed throughout their lifecycle to ensure integrity, confidentiality, availability, and traceability.

The report lifecycle shall include:

* Report Planning
* Evidence Collection
* Technical Assessment
* Report Development
* Quality Assurance Review
* Management Approval
* Report Publication
* Distribution
* Remediation Tracking
* Reassessment
* Archival
* Secure Disposal

Each phase shall be documented and governed by organizational information security policies.

---

## 21.1 Report Revision Management

Assessment reports shall be version controlled throughout their lifecycle.

Version updates shall clearly identify:

* Version number
* Revision date
* Author
* Reviewer
* Description of changes
* Approval status

Previous versions shall be retained in accordance with organizational retention requirements.

---

## 21.2 Report Change Control

Changes made after report approval shall be formally documented.

Change records should include:

* Change request identifier
* Reason for change
* Requestor
* Approver
* Date of approval
* Sections modified
* Impact assessment

Unauthorized modification of approved reports shall be prohibited.

---

Suggested metrics include:

| Metric                        | Description                                           |
| ----------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------- |
| Total Findings                | Total identified findings                             |
| Critical Findings             | Number of critical-risk findings                      |
| High Findings                 | Number of high-risk findings                          |
| Repeat Findings               | Findings recurring from previous assessments          |
| Average Risk Score            | Aggregate assessment risk rating                      |
| Evidence Completeness         | Percentage of findings supported by required evidence |
| Remediation Completion Rate   | Percentage of completed corrective actions            |
| Mean Time to Remediate (MTTR) | Average remediation duration                          |
| Assessment Coverage           | Percentage of in-scope assets assessed                |
| Report Delivery Time          | Time from assessment completion to report issuance    |

Organizations may define additional KPIs aligned with business objectives and governance requirements.

* Lessons learned
* Peer review outcomes

* Regulatory updates

EFSAF-REP-001 establishes a standardized, evidence-driven, and governance-focused approach to enterprise security assessment reporting.

By defining mandatory report structures, evidence presentation requirements, risk communication practices, remediation planning, quality assurance processes, lifecycle management, and governance controls, this standard ensures that all EFSAF assessment reports are:

* Consistent
* Accurate
* Repeatable
* Evidence-based
* Vendor-neutral
* Auditable
* Actionable
* Executive-focused
* Technically rigorous
* Suitable for regulatory and compliance review

Adherence to this standard promotes reliable decision-making, supports continuous improvement, and strengthens organizational security governance across all enterprise firewall assessment activities.
